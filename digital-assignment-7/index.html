Side Hero!

  • Originality:
    • The idea and genre behind the game is original. Side Hero is a mix between a side-scroller and a role-playing game. Although this prototype does not have all of the aspects that would be in the final version, the idea is still original. I have not played a side-scrolling RPG before either, so the genre, or mix of genres, are original as well.
    • Iteration: The idea and concept behind the game is still original. The originality above also applies to this iteration of the game.
  • Technical Merit:
    • This game has some technical merit that I have not explored before. First, the game uses animations for the player and the enemies. Specifically, the player's character has animations that interact when different inputs are given and end up interacting with each other. For instance, the player's character attacks when the mouse is clicked which causes a different animation to happen different than that of the animation that happens when the character is moving. Furthermore, I integrated a health tracking system in the game which keeps track of the player's health and the enemies health (under the hood). In this prototype, combat is initialized by the player in which if a player is close enough to an enemy and clicks to attack, then a single "round" of combat takes place between the player and the enemy. In addition, multiple forms of input are accepted by the game.
    • Iteration: Scenes were also implemented in this iteration of the game. This allows for exploration and combat to be separate in the game since combat takes place in a different scene than that of the main one. Furthermore, physics was added to the game which causes the player to jump on a couple of platforms. In relation to the physics, collisions were implemented to trigger certain things such as combat with enemies or for the player to gain a powerup. In combat, I also added some interactive buttons to do certain things in combat. I also added functionality to keep track of the player's position and place them there when they transition back to the main scene from combat.
  • Prototype Postmortem:
    • I learned about some new aspects of Phaser and laid the groundwork in this prototype for further Phaser exploration when I iterate on this prototype. For example, I learned more about Phaser animations and how they interact with each other. It is quite difficult to have a large array of animations occur on a single token, especially when the animations change based on input. In addition, I implemented a "health tracker" in the game for the player and the enemies which I had not done before, so it was interesting to think about and implement that into the game as I can now build off of that to perhaps change it to a class-like structure and build more into making a class that defines the entire character and everything associated with them. The easiest part of making the game was defining the general digital structure of the game since I had already made an analog game that related to this digital assignment. The hardest part of creating the game was dealing with the different animations of the player character since they interacted and changed frequently. In addition, it was also difficult to think about how to keep track of enemies, the player, and their attributes. Furthermore, it was difficult to learn all of the required Phaser aspects that were applicable to this game.
    • Iteration: I was able to work with scenes for the first time, so I was able to learn a lot about how scenes work and how they can switched to to create different visuals for different purposes. For example, in my game I was able to separate the main scene and combat scenes which helped organize the game better. I was also able to add physics to the game which was one of the few times I have worked with physics in Phaser. I learned a lot more about collisions and how they can be used to trigger certain events. The easiest part of the game was generating the ideas and concepts for what I wanted to do since I already had a general idea of the features that I wanted to add to the game. The hardest part of the game was making sure that the scenes worked properly since there were many small syntax issues I ran into where I was forgetting an argument to a callback function or mistyped something. The scenes were also difficult since I had not worked with them before.

  • Gameplay:
    • The gameplay starts by spawning the player character on the side-scrolling map. The goal of the game is to survive a journey through the map and then defeat the boss. The player is able to use the arrow keys and WASD to move the character side by side. In addition, the player can use the up arrow key to jump. When the player clicks, they attack. When the player character is close enough to an enemy and attacks, a "round" of combat occurs in which the player does damage first and then the enemy does damage if they were not defeated. If a player's health drops to zero or lower, they lose the game. If the player defeats the boss, then they win the game and gain the title of "Side Hero!" Some aspects of gameplay that are not in this prototype, but that I hope to include in the future iteration, include random enemy creation, ranged weapon attacks, physics that allow the player to jump and land on certain blocks, different scenes that provide turn-based combat, and powerups that the player can pick up and store in an inventory for future use.
    • Iteration: Some new gameplay aspects have been added in this iteration. First, combat is different in that when a player collides with an enemy, they are initiated into combat where they click buttons to determine what the character does to the enemy. I also added in powerups which appear to be colored squares on the map. If a player runs past a powerup, they gain its benefit. The blue powerup allows them to cast a powerful spell in combat. The teal powerup gives them the ability to add one damage to a swing in combat. The red powerup heals the player for 5 hit points. A player can see how much health and powerups they have in the top left of the screen. The player can also jump onto platforms throughout the map to reach certain enemies. Other than these changes, the gameplay is the same as the first iteration.

  • Story/Setting/Premise:
    • The game takes place in a fantasy-like, simple world in which the player is hoping to end the undead scourge that has plagued the land. Doing so grants them the title of "Side Hero." The goal of the game is to defeat the largest, most powerful undead creature in order to end the plague of undead in the land. The player character is interesting in that there are many actions that the player can take that directly impact what the player character is doing or appears to be doing. The enemies in the game are interesting because they can actually hurt the player character and take health away. This adds a level of challenge and interactivity in the game and the conflict that occurs in the game. The player token represents the player character and protagonist in the game. The mummy token represents the enemies and undead creatures that are infecting the land. The backstory is that the player is an adventurer and aspiring hero who seeks to end the undead scourge on the land. They have travelled to the center of the undead territory to defeat the largest and most powerful undead leader or boss. The dramatic tension of the game connects to the gameplay tension of the game in that the player character is trying to survive and defeat the boss, but also encounters smaller enemies that can hinder their progress. As a result, the player's dramatic, over-arching goal can be stopped short if they are not careful with the gameplay choices they make while creating the game.
